Pinoy Embutido

Pinoy embutido is a Filipino-style meatloaf often served during special occasions or festivities. It's made by mixing ground pork with various ingredients such as eggs, breadcrumbs, carrots, bell peppers, raisins, and sometimes cheese and pickles. The mixture is then wrapped tightly in aluminum foil or plastic wrap, steamed or baked until cooked through, and sliced before serving. It's typically enjoyed with banana ketchup or a sweet and tangy sauce.

Pinoy Embutido Preparation

Preparation time:
15 minutes

Cooking time:
1 hour

Total time:
1 hour and 15 minutes

Number of servings:
4

Ingredients:

  • • 1 kg ground pork.
  • • 1 cup finely chopped carrots.
  • • 1 cup finely chopped bell peppers (red and green).
  • • 1 cup finely chopped onions.
  • • 1 cup breadcrumbs.
  • • 3 eggs, beaten.
  • • ½ cup raisins.
  • • ½ cup sweet pickle relish.
  • • ¼ cup tomato sauce.
  • • 2 tablespoons soy sauce.
  • • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce.
  • • Salt and pepper to taste.

Directions

Step 1

Prepare the Meat Mixture

In a large mixing bowl, combine 1 kg ground pork, 1 cup finely chopped carrots, 1 cup finely chopped bell peppers (red and green), 1 cup finely chopped onions, 1 cup breadcrumbs, 3 eggs (beaten), ½ cup raisins, ½ cup sweet pickle relish, ¼ cup tomato sauce, 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly until well combined. You can use your hands for this step to ensure even distribution of ingredients.

Step 2

Assemble the Embutido

Prepare sheets of aluminum foil or cling film for wrapping the embutido. Divide the meat mixture into portions depending on the size of embutido you prefer. Place a portion of the meat mixture onto a sheet of aluminum foil or cling film and shape it into a log or roll. Roll it tightly to remove air pockets and achieve a compact shape. Roll the aluminum foil or cling film tightly around the meat mixture, twisting the ends to seal it securely. Repeat the process until all the meat mixture is used.

Step 3

Steam the Embutido

Arrange the wrapped embutido in a steamer basket, leaving space between each roll to allow steam to circulate. Steam the embutido over boiling water for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is fully cooked through. Once cooked, carefully remove the embutido from the steamer and let them cool slightly before unwrapping.

Step 4

Serve and Enjoy!

Unwrap the embutido from the aluminum foil or cling film and slice them into rounds. Arrange the sliced embutido on a serving platter. Serve warm or at room temperature with your favorite dipping sauce, such as banana ketchup or sweet chili sauce. Enjoy your homemade embutido as a delicious appetizer, snack, or main dish!
