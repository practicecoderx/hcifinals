Ginataang Hipon

Ginataang Hipon is a traditional Filipino dish featuring shrimp cooked in coconut milk (gata). It's a creamy and savory dish that typically includes garlic, onions, ginger, and sometimes chili peppers for a hint of spice. The shrimp are simmered in the coconut milk until cooked through and flavorful, creating a delicious and comforting seafood dish that's often enjoyed with steamed rice. Other variations may include additional ingredients such as vegetables like squash or spinach to add more depth to the dish.

Ginataang Hipon Preparation

Preparation time:
15 minutes

Cooking time:
20 minutes

Total time:
35 minutes

Number of servings:
4

Ingredients:

  • • 500 grams large shrimp (hipon), peeled and deveined.
  • • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped.
  • • 3 cloves garlic, minced.
  • • 1-2 pieces red chili (siling labuyo), sliced (optional).
  • • 2 cups coconut milk.
  • • 1 tablespoon cooking oil.
  • • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • • Additional vegetables (optional).

Directions

Step 1

Prepare the Ingredients

Peel and devein 500 grams of large shrimp (hipon). You can leave the heads and tails intact for presentation, if desired. Chop 1 medium-sized onion and mince 3 cloves of garlic. Slice 1-2 pieces of red chili (siling labuyo) for a spicy kick (optional). Measure 2 cups of coconut milk. You can use canned coconut milk or freshly extracted coconut milk from grated coconut. Prepare additional vegetables if desired, such as sliced eggplant, spinach, or green beans.

Step 2

Sauté Aromatics

Heat a tablespoon of cooking oil in a large skillet or pan over medium heat. Sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant and the onions are translucent. If you're using chili peppers for spice, add them to the skillet and sauté briefly.

Step 3

Add Shrimp and Coconut Milk

Once the aromatics are sautéed, add the peeled and deveined shrimp to the skillet. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. Pour in 2 cups of coconut milk into the skillet. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

Step 4

Simmer and Serve

Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Allow it to cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the shrimp are fully cooked and the coconut milk has thickened slightly. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper according to your preference. If you're adding additional vegetables like eggplant or spinach, add them to the skillet during the last few minutes of cooking to allow them to cook through. Once the shrimp are cooked and the sauce has thickened to your liking, remove the skillet from heat. Transfer the Ginataang Hipon to a serving dish and serve hot with steamed rice.
