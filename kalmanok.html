CHOSEN DISH




Kalderetang Manok

Kalderetang Manok is a Filipino chicken stew that shares similarities with the more common beef version, Kaldereta. In this dish, chicken pieces are simmered in a flavorful tomato-based sauce along with vegetables such as potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers. The sauce is typically rich and tangy, infused with the flavors of garlic, onions, and spices like bay leaves and black peppercorns. Liver spread or liver paste is often added to enhance the depth of flavor. Kalderetang Manok is a hearty and satisfying dish, commonly served with steamed rice for a comforting meal.

Kalderetang Manok

Preparation time:
10 minutes

Cooking time:
55 minutes

Total time:
1 hour and 5 minutes

Number of servings:
4

Ingredients:

  • • Chicken pieces (about 1.5 kg), preferably with bone-in for flavor.
  • • 2-3 tablespoons cooking oil.
  • • 1 large onion, chopped.
  • • 4 cloves garlic, minced.
  • • 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed.
  • • 1 large carrot, peeled and sliced.
  • • 1 red bell pepper, sliced.
  • • 1 green bell pepper, sliced.
  • • 1 cup tomato sauce.
  • • 2 tablespoons liver spread (optional but traditional).
  • • 1 cup chicken broth or water.
  • • 2 tablespoons soy sauce.
  • • 1 tablespoon vinegar.
  • • 1 tablespoon sugar.
  • • 1 bay leaf.
  • • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • • Siling labuyo or chili flakes (optional, for added heat.)
  • • Green peas (optional, for garnish).

Directions

Step 1

Sauté Aromatics and Chicken

Heat 2-3 tablespoons of cooking oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Sauté 1 chopped large onion and 4 cloves minced garlic until fragrant and translucent. Add the chicken pieces (about 1.5 kg) to the pot. You can use a mix of chicken thighs, drumsticks, or breast pieces. Cook until the chicken is lightly browned on all sides.

Step 2

Add Vegetables and Sauce

Once the chicken is browned, add 2 large peeled and cubed potatoes, 1 large peeled and sliced carrot, 1 sliced red bell pepper, and 1 sliced green bell pepper to the pot. Pour in 1 cup of tomato sauce, 2 tablespoons of liver spread (optional but traditional), and 1 cup of chicken broth or water. Stir in 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of vinegar, 1 tablespoon of sugar, 1 bay leaf, and season with salt and pepper to taste. If you like it spicy, you can add siling labuyo (bird's eye chili) or chili flakes at this point.

Step 3

Simmer Until Chicken is Tender and Ready to Serve

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender, and the vegetables are soft. Occasionally stir the stew to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
