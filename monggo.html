Monggo

Monggo (or Munggo) is a classic Filipino dish made from mung beans, a type of legume commonly used in Asian cuisine. The dish typically starts by simmering mung beans with garlic, onions, and tomatoes until they become soft and creamy. Various meats such as pork, chicken, or fish can be added to the stew for extra flavor and protein. It's often seasoned with fish sauce or salt and pepper, and sometimes includes leafy greens like spinach or bitter melon leaves. Monggo is a nutritious and comforting dish enjoyed throughout the Philippines, often served with rice as a main meal.

Monggo Preparation

Preparation time:
5 minutes

Cooking time:
45 minutes

Total time:
50 minutes

Number of servings:
4

Ingredients:

  • • 1 cup mung beans.
  • • 6 ounces pork, sliced into thin small pieces.
  • • 1 Knorr pork cube.
  • • 1 bunch spinach, washed.
  • • 1 cup malunggay leaves.
  • • 2 medium tomatoes, cubed.
  • • 1 medium onion, cubed.
  • • 1/2 cup chicharon, crumbled.
  • • 4 cloves garlic, chopped into small pieces.
  • • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper.
  • • 1 to 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis).
  • • 6 cups water.
  • • 3 tablespoons cooking oil.

Directions

Step 1

Heat the oil

Heat oil in a cooking pot. Saute garlic, onion, and tomato. Add the pork. Cook until the color of the pork turns light brown.

Step 2

Pour water

Pour water into the pot. Let boil and add the pork cube. Stir. Add the mung beans. Cover and adjust the heat between low to medium. Continue to cook until the mung beans becomes tender.

Step 3

Stir and cook

Put the malunggay leaves and spinach in the cooking pot. Stir and cook for 2 minutes. Add ground black pepper and fish sauce. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes.

Step 4

Serve and Enjoy!

Transfer to a serving bowl. Top with chicharon. Serve, Share and enjoy!
