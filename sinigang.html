Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is a popular Filipino sour soup made with pork and a variety of vegetables, simmered in a sour broth typically flavored with tamarind. The pork is often stewed until tender along with vegetables like tomatoes, radish, eggplant, and green beans. Other souring agents such as calamansi, guava, or green mango can also be used depending on regional preferences. The sourness of the broth is balanced with savory flavors from the pork and the umami richness of fish sauce. Sinigang na Baboy is a comforting and flavorful dish commonly enjoyed with steamed rice, especially during colder or rainy days.

Sinigang na Baboy Preparation

Preparation time:
10 minutes

Cooking time:
1 hour and 30 minutes

Total time:
1 hour and 40 minutes

Number of servings:
6

Ingredients:

  • • 2 lbs. pork belly, sliced into cubes.
  • • 2 to 3 cups kangkong leaves.
  • • 3 pieces long green chili pepper (siling pansigang).
  • • 2 pieces tomato, quartered.
  • • 1 cup sliced daikon radish (labanos).
  • • 10 to 12 pieces snake beans, cut in 2 inch length pieces.
  • • 1 piece onion, quartered.
  • • 15 pieces okra.
  • • 2 pieces taro, peeled and cut in half (gabi).
  • • 8 cups water.
  • • 2 packs Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix Original (20 gram pack).
  • • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (patis).
  • • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper.

Directions

Step 1

Cook the ingredients

Start to cook sinigang by boiling onions and tomato. This provides flavor to the broth. Do this by boiling water in a wide and deep cooking pot. Add these ingredients after the water boils.

Step 2

Cook the pork belly

Pork belly goes into the pot. Cook it for 25 to 30 minutes between low to medium heat. Add the taro and continue to boil in medium heat for 20 to 30 minutes or until the pork gets very tender.

Step 3

Add the Knorr Cubes

Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc recipe mix afterwards.

Step 4

Serve and Enjoy!

Let the radish cook for 5 minutes before adding all the remaining vegetables. Then you can enjoy the hot served sinigang na manok.
