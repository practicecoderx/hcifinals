Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish originating from the Bicol region in the Philippines. It's known for its spicy and flavorful taste, typically made with pork cooked in coconut milk, shrimp paste (bagoong), garlic, onions, and plenty of chili peppers, particularly bird's eye chili or "siling labuyo". The dish's name is derived from the train service that used to run from Manila to Bicol, suggesting its supposed spicy nature can make you feel like you're on an "express" train ride.

Bicol Express Preparation

Preparation time:
5 minutes

Cooking time:
55 minutes

Total time:
1 hour

Number of servings:
6

Ingredients:

  • • 2 lbs. pork belly, sliced into strips.
  • • 2 cups coconut milk.
  • • 2 cups coconut cream.
  • • 1/2 cup shrimp paste (bagoong alamang).
  • • 4 cloves garlic, crushed.
  • • 6 pieces Thai chili pepper, chopped.
  • • 1 thumb ginger, minced.
  • • 1 piece onion, chopped.
  • • 1 piece Serrano pepper, sliced.
  • • 1 cup water (optional).

Directions

Step 1

Prepare the ingredients

Start by taking out your pan. Proceed by combining minced ginger, crushed garlic, chopped onion, sliced pork, chopped chili pepper, and coconut milk.

Step 2

Mix

Mix everything together before turning-on the heat.

Step 3

Cook in Low Heat

Once the mixture boils, you may add a cup of coconut cream and half of the bagoong alamang. Let the mixture cook using low heat until it reduces to a quarter.

Step 4

Serve and Enjoy!

Continue cooking in low heat until the sauce thickens. Make sure to serve this nice and hot, and you now have enough Bicol Express for 6! Also try serving it the traditional manner– with some steaming, white rice.
