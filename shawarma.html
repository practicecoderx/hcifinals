Pinoy Shawarma

"Pinoy Shawarma" is a Filipino adaptation of the Middle Eastern shawarma, which has been localized to suit Filipino tastes. It typically consists of thinly sliced marinated meat, such as beef, chicken, or pork, cooked on a vertical rotisserie or grill. The meat is then wrapped in a soft pita bread or flatbread and served with various condiments and sauces, such as garlic yogurt sauce, cucumber, tomatoes, onions, and lettuce. Pinoy Shawarma often incorporates Filipino flavors and spices into the marinade and sauces, giving it a unique twist compared to traditional Middle Eastern shawarma. It has become a popular street food and fast-food option in the Philippines, offering a delicious blend of international and local flavors.

Pinoy Shawarma Preparation

Preparation time:
30 minutes

Cooking time:
20 minutes

Total time:
50 minutes

Number of servings:
3

Ingredients:

  • • 500 grams boneless chicken thighs or beef sirloin.
  • • ½ cup plain yogurt.
  • • 2 tablespoons vinegar (preferably apple cider vinegar).
  • • 2 cloves garlic, minced.
  • • 1 teaspoon ground cumin.
  • • 1 teaspoon paprika.
  • • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric.
  • • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper.
  • • Salt, to taste.
  • • Pita bread or flatbread.
  • • Toppings (tomatoes, onions, cucumber, lettuce, pickles).
  • • Sauce (tahini sauce or garlic yogurt sauce).

Directions

Step 1

Prepare the Marinade

In a bowl, combine ½ cup of plain yogurt, 2 tablespoons of vinegar (preferably apple cider vinegar), 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of ground cumin, 1 teaspoon of paprika, ½ teaspoon of ground turmeric, ½ teaspoon of ground black pepper, and salt to taste. Mix well until the marinade is smooth and all ingredients are fully incorporated.

Step 2

Marinate the Meat

Thinly slice 500 grams of boneless chicken thighs or beef sirloin. Place the sliced meat into the marinade, ensuring each piece is coated evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight for best flavor.

Step 3

Cook the Shawarma

Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Remove the marinated meat from the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature. Once the skillet is hot, add a drizzle of oil. Cook the marinated meat in batches until fully cooked and slightly charred on the edges, about 5-7 minutes per batch.

Step 4

Serve and Enjoy!

Warm some pita bread or flatbread in a dry skillet or microwave. Place a generous amount of the cooked shawarma meat onto the center of the warmed bread. Add your desired toppings such as sliced tomatoes, onions, cucumber, lettuce, and pickles. Drizzle with your favorite sauce, such as tahini sauce or garlic yogurt sauce. Roll the bread tightly around the filling to form a wrap or shawarma sandwich.
