Kare-Kare

Kare-kare is a traditional Filipino stew characterized by its rich and savory peanut sauce. It's typically made with tender cuts of oxtail, tripe, and/or beef stewed until tender, then simmered in a thick peanut sauce made from ground roasted peanuts and rice flour. Vegetables such as eggplant, banana blossom, and string beans are often added to the stew for texture and flavor. Kare-kare is commonly served with shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side and enjoyed with rice. It's a beloved dish in Filipino cuisine, often served during special occasions and family gatherings.

Kare-Kare Preparation

Preparation time:
10 minutes

Cooking time:
2 hours 30 minutes

Total time:
2 hours 40 minutes

Number of servings:
6

Ingredients:

  • • 3 lbs oxtail, cut in 2 inch slices (you can also use tripe or beef slices).
  • • 1 piece small banana flower bud, sliced.
  • • 1 bundle pechay or bok choy.
  • • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2 inch slices.
  • • 4 pieces eggplants, sliced.
  • • 1 cup ground peanuts.
  • • 1/2 cup peanut butter.
  • • 1/2 cup shrimp paste.
  • • 34 ounces water (about 1 Liter).
  • • 1/2 cup annatto seeds, soaked in a cup of water.
  • • 1/2 cup toasted ground rice.
  • • 1 tbsp garlic, minced.
  • • 1 piece onion, chopped.
  • • Salt and pepper.

Directions

Step 1

Simmer the ingredients

In a large pot, bring the water to a boil. Put in the oxtail followed by the onions and simmer for 2.5 to 3 hrs or until tender

Step 2

Add ground rice

Once the meat is tender, add the ground peanuts, peanut butter, and coloring. Add the toasted ground rice and simmer for 5 minutes

Step 3

Cook on a separate pan

On a separate pan, saute the garlic then add the banana flower, eggplant, and string beans and cook for 5 minutes. Transfer the cooked vegetables to the large pot

Step 4

Serve and Enjoy!

Add salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with shrimp paste. Enjoy!
